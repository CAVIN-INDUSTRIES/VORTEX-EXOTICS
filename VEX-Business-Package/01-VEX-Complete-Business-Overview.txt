TERMS USED IN THIS DOCUMENT (QUICK GLOSSARY)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  CRM              Customer relationship management — software dealers use to
                   track shoppers, vehicles, deals, and follow-ups.
  Subscription     Customers pay a recurring fee (monthly or yearly) to use the
                   platform — similar to Netflix, but for dealer software.
  Stripe           A widely used payment and billing platform; VEX integrates
                   with it for checkout and subscriptions.
  API              Application programming interface — a secure, structured way
                   for the website, dealer tools, and other systems to talk to
                   the same backend data without mixing one dealer with another.
  Multi-tenant     Each dealer’s data is separated logically so one account
                   cannot see another’s private information.
  Pilot program    Early partners who go live on the platform under a defined
                   onboarding and verification process.

EXECUTIVE SNAPSHOT (PLAIN ENGLISH)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
**VEX (Vortex Exotic Exchange)** is building a **single, luxury-focused digital
system** for high-end car dealers and their buyers:

  • **Buyer-facing side:** A premium website experience — rich visuals, optional
    3D configuration (“build your car” online), and a marketplace feel suited to
    exotic and luxury inventory rather than a generic used-car template.

  • **Dealer-facing side:** A unified **dashboard** (CRM) where staff manage
    inventory, customer conversations, appraisals, trade-ins, and deal progress
    in one place instead of juggling disconnected tools.

  • **Under the hood:** One **secure technical foundation** (multi-tenant API)
    with **Stripe**-ready billing, so growth can scale without reinventing
    payments each time.

**Brand line (approved marketing language):**
  “One obsidian vault. One glass cockpit. One breathtaking digital-twin
   experience. The domain that draws global attention like no other.”

  In everyday terms: the public site should feel like a **private, high-end
  showroom**; the dealer tools should feel like a **single glass cockpit** for
  running the business; the experience should feel as polished as the cars.

1. THE PROBLEM AND THE OPPORTUNITY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Challenge                         What it means in practice
  --------------------------------  ------------------------------------------
  Mass-market tools optimize        Big retail auto stacks are built for
  volume, not emotion               volume and efficiency — not the “desire”
                                    and trust luxury buyers expect online.
  Fragmented dealer software        Staff copy data between systems; deals
                                    slow down; the brand feels inconsistent.
  Weak handoff from web to desk     A shopper configures or inquires online,
                                    but the showroom never gets a clean story.

**Opportunity:** Own the **luxury and exotic** slice with one coherent product
— stunning public presence **and** serious dealer operations — instead of a
patchwork of generic sites and bolt-on tools.


2. WHAT THE PRODUCT INCLUDES (LAYMAN’S TOUR)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
**For car shoppers (public website)**
  • A **cinematic** first impression — dark, premium “vault” styling, strong
    visuals, smooth motion where hardware allows (with sensible fallbacks for
    accessibility and older devices).
  • **3D configuration** where deployed — pick finishes and options in the
    browser, closer to a digital twin of the car than a flat brochure.
  • **Apex Studio**-style experiences (where rolled out) for flagship builds.
  • **Marketplace-style** browsing — vehicle detail pages, paths to financing
    interest, and contact with the dealer.

**For dealership staff (internal tools)**
  • **CRM / “glass cockpit”** — pipeline, customers, vehicles, orders.
  • **Appraisals and trade-ins** — including public lead forms where enabled,
    plus valuation providers and sensible fallbacks (subject to compliance).
  • **Financing and shipping** — user flows and partner hooks, configured per
    deployment.

**Platform and trust**
  • **Separation between dealers** (multi-tenant) and **role-based access**
    (who can see or change what).
  • **Stripe** subscriptions, checkout patterns, and webhooks (automated
    billing events) implemented in line with product standards.
  • **Pilot onboarding** — branded domains, checklists, and verification so
    go-live is repeatable, not improvised.

5. NUMBERS YOU CAN TREAT AS “REAL” (WHEN EXPORTED FROM THE LIVE SYSTEM)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
When pulled from **production** (the live deployed product), the following are
grounded in code and data — not invented in this text file:

  • **Published list prices** — exposed as “plans” the app can show publicly.
  • **Per-dealer MRR snapshot** — a simplified “if this account is on an active
    subscription, what tier price does that imply per month?” view for investor
    or capital workflows (not the same as full accounting revenue).
  • **Pilot network statistics** — counts derived from the database, such as
    how many pilot dealers are onboarded, appraisal activity, and closed orders
    under defined rules.

Anything not exported from those systems on a given date is **not certified**
in this package.


6. COMPETITION (BIG PICTURE, NO JARGON)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The largest automotive retail technology companies are built for **national
scale** and deep operational plumbing (inventory networks, auctions, mass-market
listings). VEX does **not** claim to replace that entire world overnight.

VEX’s wedge is **depth and emotion for luxury and exotic retail** — a unified,
premium digital layer for buyers and staff — rather than winning on sheer
corporate breadth.


7. GO-TO-MARKET (HOW VEX SELLS AND ONBOARDS)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Focus on **luxury and exotic** dealers, **white-label** domains where appropriate,
and **verified** launch checklists. Demos should tell one story: **same brand,
same system** — from the public hero experience through configuration into the
dealer dashboard.
