WHAT THIS DOCUMENT IS (AND IS NOT)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This report does three things:

  1. Lists **subscription list prices** that are **actually coded** into the VEX
     product today (so you can verify them in the live app or source file).

  2. Explains, in plain language, **how the software computes a simple monthly
     recurring revenue (MRR) snapshot** for certain investor-style screens —
     and why that is **not** the same as full audited revenue.

  3. Provides a **blank worksheet** so your **finance or accounting** team can
     build real forecasts from **Stripe**, your general ledger, and contracts.

This is **not** an audit, a securities filing, or a promise of future results.
Older drafts of marketing packages sometimes showed invented “year 5 ARR” curves;
**do not** treat those as facts. This file removes that guesswork from the
written package.

TERMS USED HERE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  MRR (monthly recurring revenue)  The subscription dollars you would expect
                                   per month from active software plans — a
                                   common SaaS metric (SaaS = software sold as
                                   a subscription).
  ARR (annual recurring revenue)   Roughly MRR × 12 when people annualize.
  Stripe                           Payment and billing platform; what customers
                                   actually pay must match Stripe’s live prices
                                   if those differ from internal list tables.
  API                              The structured way the app publishes data
                                   (for example “current plan prices”) to the
                                   website and tools.
  GAAP                             Generally accepted accounting principles —
                                   the rules accountants use; product “MRR”
                                   snapshots may not equal GAAP revenue without
                                   adjustment.
  Tenant                           One dealer organization’s isolated account
                                   in the system.

SECTION 2 — HOW MRR IS CALCULATED IN SOFTWARE (SINGLE DEALER SNAPSHOT)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
File: apps/api/src/routes/capital.ts

In simple terms:

  • Each subscription tier maps to a **monthly dollar amount**:
      Pro → $149/mo, Enterprise → $299/mo, Starter → $49/mo by default.

  • If that dealer account shows an **active** paid subscription in Stripe’s
    status (not canceled), the system records that dollar amount as **mrr**
    for certain **capital / investor** style responses.

  • If there is no active subscription, **mrr is zero** in that snapshot.

**Why finance still matters:** Real revenue recognition can include discounts,
refunds, taxes, timing of invoices, and contract wording. Treat this **mrr**
field as a **product indicator**, then reconcile to **Stripe reports** and your
**books**.


SECTION 3 — LIVE DATA YOU CAN REQUEST FROM THE RUNNING PRODUCT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
These are **technical endpoints** (URLs on your API) — your engineering or ops
team can help you call them. Listed here so finance knows **where truth lives**.

  • **Current list prices** — public; no login required on typical setups.
      GET /pricing/plans

  • **Raise package (per logged-in dealer admin)** — includes mrr and usage
    summaries scoped to that dealer’s account.
      GET /capital/package   (requires admin authentication)

  • **Time-limited investor link** — creates a token a reader can use briefly.
      POST /capital/investor-link
      GET /capital/investor/:token

  • **Pilot network rollups** — counts computed from the database, defined in:
      apps/api/src/lib/pilotMetrics.ts

**Pilot-style metrics (plain English):**

  activePilots
    How many dealer accounts are flagged as having completed pilot onboarding.

  totalPilotAppraisals
    Appraisal records tied to those pilot dealers.

  firstBillingEvents
    How many pilot dealers have a Stripe customer record on file (a billing
    readiness signal — not the same as total lifetime cash).

  publicIntakeToday / publicQuickAppraisalSubmissionsLifetime
    Counts of public appraisal form usage from usage logs.

  closedDealsAcrossPilots
    Orders marked confirmed or fulfilled for pilot dealers.

  generatedAt
    Timestamp showing when the rollup was computed.

**Company-wide MRR:** If your deployment includes an admin MRR dashboard or
**GET /admin/mrr**, use **production exports** from there for totals — not this
static file.


SECTION 4 — DEMO / PLACEHOLDER VALUES (DO NOT QUOTE AS REAL FINANCIALS)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Some API responses include **hard-coded example numbers** so screens and demos
work before final data exists. Examples in **capital.ts** include:

  • A sample **monthly burn** dollar figure inside a “Series A data room” helper.
  • A sample **month-over-month growth** heuristic derived from usage vs MRR.
  • A placeholder **churn percentage** on an investor-style “live” route.
  • Default **valuation** and **raise amount** inputs on a term-sheet simulator.

These are **scaffolding**. They are **not** your company’s financial statements
unless leadership deliberately replaces them with audited inputs.


SECTION 5 — INTERNAL PLANNING DOCS VS THE LIVE PRICE LIST
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Long internal specs (markdown files under docs/plans/) sometimes mention
**illustrative** prices for storytelling — for example a future “premium tier”
price that was never shipped.

**Rule of thumb:** For customers, investors doing diligence, or legal documents,
use **GET /pricing/plans**, **Stripe**, or your **signed price book** — not a
planning memo.
